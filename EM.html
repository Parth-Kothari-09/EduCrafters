A system of forces is a collection of forces acting on an object simultaneously. Any external agent that changes or tries to change an object’s state is called a force. A force requires four characteristics for representation: magnitude, direction, point of application, and line of action. The unit of force in the SI system is “Newton”. In real life, many objects are subjected to more than one force, i.e., a system of forces. The analysis of such objects becomes a bit complicated.

When one body interacts with another, they exert different forces on each other. When multiple forces act on a body at the same time, they form a system known as a force system or system of force. In simple words, the system of force can be defined as a force system or system of force is a group of forces acting on a body or number of connected bodies. As a result, it is a term used to describe a group of forces. Forces can act on a single point or in a single plane. Forces can also act on several planes of a three-dimensional object. Some real-life applications for the system of forces are: forces on the tug of war, forces on blades of ceiling fans, forces at a joint of truss, etc.
